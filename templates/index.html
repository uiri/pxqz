PXQZ


There's a commandline tool written in perl. It takes the paste on stdin and gives you a url on stdout. The url points to the paste, served as text/plain. You can access it here or just run 
curl p.xqz.ca/script > pxqz;chmod 755 pxqz; su -c "mv pxqz /usr/local/bin"
to download it and put it in your path.

In addition to the commandline tool written in perl, there is an emacs minor mode to make it easy to post a buffer using "C-x p". It is available here; easily downloadable into one's .emacs.d just like every other paste.
